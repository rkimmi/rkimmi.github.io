What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

"A beautiful experience" were his comments. We focussed initally on my mindfulness listening, and he said it was really nice to have an opportunity to externalise his thoughts. I learnt a lot more about my friend and was thankful for him being so open about his then current state of mind.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

The option to 'do less than you can' is something that suits me, so that I don't lose interest.

Any other musings?

I'm wondering why more emphasis is not placed on the ethical responsibility of the mindful listener, that by offering space for people to share their thoughts and often vulnerabilities they should be held accountable to nurture that information in some way. This may seem off topic, but I have known people to be invested in similar rhetoric only to covertly use this information as power. Maybe this is exclusive to someone pure evil, but I am skeptical.
