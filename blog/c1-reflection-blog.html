What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

EDA's education ethos emphasises personal collaborative working relationships, which I think is one of its most credible differences to our standard education models.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I initially thought that I'd engage with and fit into this culture quite well but it's proven to be more difficult, as I'm realising that my skills in communication aren't that great, e.g; I'm not always easy to contact and often forget to reply to messages on Slack (whoops), also being honest about both my technical and personal struggles is sometimes a hard to do.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

My expectations of EDA have shifted in that I understand EDA to be at this point 'not just' providing skills in wed dev but also the collaborative and interpersonal skills that this work requires.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am both nervous and excited to participate within the learning environment of EDA :)
