What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

EDA's education ethos emphasises personal collaborative working relationships, which I think is one of its most credible differences to the impersonal, beurocratic systems of tertiary systems in Aotearoa currently.

What are your impressions?

My impressions so far working within the EDA programme are positive, although I am beginning to experience more so the mental and emotional challenges/changes that I had been forewarned about coming in to the programme.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

In Sheeraf's fireside chat he spoke of an 'educational trauma' which I relate very much to. I have been recognising my own behaviour acquired from working within educational systems that I feel are oppressive — these being behaviours such as stubbornness and criticality which I am trying not to default to while at EDA. I see myself having to 'let my guard down' as to fully participate in and benefit from the culture at EDA.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

My expectations of EDA have shifted in that I understand EDA to be 'not just' an education provider.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am somewhat nervous to participate within the learning environment of EDA but am more so thankful for the opportunity. I'm hoping to become less nervous progressing through Phase 0.
